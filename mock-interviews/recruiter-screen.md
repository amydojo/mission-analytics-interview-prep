# Recruiter Phone-Screen Mock

## Objective

Simulate a 25 to 30 minute recruiter screen. The recruiter is deciding whether Josh's background is relevant, credible, logistically aligned, and worth technical-team time.

## Instructions for Amy

- Set a timer for 28 minutes.
- Record audio.
- Stay neutral and professional.
- Do not coach during an answer.
- Allow one natural follow-up when an answer is vague.
- Interrupt only when an answer exceeds its time budget.
- Debrief using [`feedback-log.md`](feedback-log.md) after the complete mock.

## Time budgets

| Answer type | Target |
|---|---:|
| Tell me about yourself | 60 to 90 seconds |
| Motivation | 45 to 60 seconds |
| Project story | 90 seconds |
| Gap or logistics | 20 to 40 seconds |
| Candidate question | 20 seconds plus discussion |

## Recruiter opening

> Thanks for taking the time today. I would like to learn more about your background, tell you a little about the role, and leave time for your questions.

## Core screen

1. Walk me through your background.
2. What interested you in Anduril?
3. Why this Mission Analytics Engineer role?
4. What is the most relevant work you have done for this position?
5. Tell me more about the RF traffic defect on your résumé.
6. What did you personally own?
7. How did you validate that the result was correct?
8. Your titles were systems and software engineer. How does your experience meet the three-year data-oriented requirement?
9. What cloud data solutions have you contributed to?
10. Have you used Spark, Kafka, Flink, dbt, Foundry, or similar tools?
11. What have you been doing since Viasat?
12. Why are you looking to leave teaching?
13. Why did you leave Viasat?
14. What is your current clearance status?
15. Are you authorized to work in the United States without sponsorship?
16. Are you comfortable working onsite in Irvine?
17. Are you comfortable with occasional travel?
18. What compensation range are you considering?
19. Where are you in your job search, and what is your timing?
20. What questions do you have for me?

## Objection round

Use two of these after the core screen:

> I see strong systems experience, but less direct experience with a modern analytics stack. Why should the team move you forward?

> The role requires cloud participation, and that is not obvious from your résumé. Can you clarify?

> You have been away from a full-time engineering role since 2023. How have you kept your skills current?

> This role may involve classified and network-isolated environments. What relevant experience do you have?

> Why are you applying to this instead of a conventional data scientist position?

## Pressure follow-ups

- What was the exact scale?
- What was the input and output?
- What did you personally implement?
- How did you know the data was wrong?
- What alternative explanation did you rule out?
- What was the root cause?
- How was the improvement measured?
- What would you do differently now?
- Can you explain that without the acronym?
- Which part of that work is most transferable here?

## Candidate questions

Josh should choose three:

1. What aspects of my background stood out when the team reviewed my application?
2. How is this role divided between data infrastructure, system-performance investigation, and field support?
3. What would the next interview stage evaluate?
4. What are the first problems the person hired would tackle?
5. How does the team define success in the first six months?

## Strong closing

> Thank you. The combination of systems performance, trusted data, and defense operations is exactly what made the role stand out to me. I appreciate the context on the team and next steps.

## Debrief questions

- Could the recruiter summarize Josh's fit in one sentence?
- Did Josh make his data-oriented experience obvious?
- Did he defend the 100% result?
- Did he answer cloud experience precisely?
- Did he sound interested in this role rather than merely unemployed?
- Were logistics answered without hesitation?
- Did any answer create a new concern?
