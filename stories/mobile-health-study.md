## Recruiter-level version

> In my master's program, I analyzed repeated mobile-health observations from about 300 participants. Because each participant contributed multiple days of data, ordinary regression would have treated dependent observations as independent. I used mixed-effects models to separate individual baselines from differences in treatment response and translated the results into where tailored messaging appeared more useful than generic messaging.
