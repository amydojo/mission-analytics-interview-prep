# Tell Me About Yourself

## Goal

Create a coherent 60 to 90 second narrative that makes Josh feel like an obvious mission-analytics candidate, not a generic data scientist.

## Recommended answer

> I am a systems and software engineer with a bachelor's in electrical engineering and a master's in statistics. My background has primarily been at the intersection of software, RF systems, automation, and data analysis.
>
> At Raytheon, I developed software for RF simulation and worked in a classified aerospace environment under a Secret clearance. At Viasat, I built Python automation for CI/CD pipelines and created an analytical model using SQL queries of RF test data to predict network traffic. That work helped me identify a defect that was causing measured traffic to be overestimated by 100%.
>
> I completed my master's in statistics to deepen the analytical side of my background. I am interested in this role because it combines the areas where I am strongest: understanding complex system behavior, building trustworthy analytical tools, performing root-cause analysis, and supporting real-world defense systems.

## 30-second version

> I am a systems and software engineer with defense, RF, Python, SQL, and graduate statistics experience. At Viasat, I built an analytical model that exposed a defect causing a 100% overestimation in network traffic, and at Raytheon I developed RF simulation software in a classified aerospace environment. This role is compelling because it brings systems engineering and trusted operational analytics together.

## Delivery rules

- Lead with professional identity, not education chronology.
- Pause after the 100% result.
- Say what Josh personally did.
- Do not list every skill.
- Do not call this a career pivot.
- End with a direct link to the role.
