## Honest adjacency answer

> I have not yet owned a large cloud-native data platform in production. My closest experience includes Python and SQL analytical workflows, API-driven system migrations, CI/CD automation, and Linux-based production systems. I understand the major architecture concerns, and I would rather be exact about my current depth than overstate it. I have a strong record of learning complex systems quickly and validating my work in environments where reliability matters.

## Follow-up questions to expect

- Which AWS, GCP, or Azure services did you use?
- What did you personally configure?
- How did data enter and leave the system?
- How were credentials and access handled?
- How was it deployed and monitored?
- What would change at larger scale?

## Red flags

- Naming AWS or GCP without a service or use case
- Presenting certification coursework as production ownership
- Saying only "I am a fast learner"
- Bluffing architecture details
- Becoming apologetic and abandoning the evidence from Viasat

## Pivot back to strength

> The specific managed service may be new, but tracing data lineage, validating system outputs, automating reliable workflows, and debugging failures across system boundaries are not new problems for me.
