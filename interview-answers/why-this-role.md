# Why This Role?

## Recommended answer

> I enjoy statistical modeling, but my strongest work has always combined analysis with systems engineering. I like understanding where data came from, whether it can be trusted, how the surrounding system behaves, and how the result affects an operational decision.
>
> This Mission Analytics Engineer role brings those pieces together. It involves building reliable data systems, investigating production performance, supporting hardware and field teams, and creating reusable analytics products. That is much closer to the work I have found most meaningful than a role focused only on model development or reporting.

## Evidence to attach

- Viasat analytical model based on SQL queries of RF test data
- Root-cause analysis of 100% traffic overestimation
- Python CI/CD automation and reliability improvement
- Raytheon RF simulation in a classified environment
- Finisar hardware verification automation
- M.S. Statistics for formal analytical depth

## One-line version

> This role gives the right name to the work I have already been doing at the intersection of systems, data, and operational reliability.

## Follow-up: Why not a conventional data scientist role?

> I value modeling, but I am most effective when analysis stays connected to the source system, data quality, engineering constraints, and the decision being made. Mission analytics preserves that end-to-end ownership.
